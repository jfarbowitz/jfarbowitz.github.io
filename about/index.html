About

Jonathan Farbowitz a graduate of New York University’s Moving Image Archiving and Preservation (MIAP) program. His interests include preserving malware and other digital anomalies, archiving media related to social movements, the ethical treatment of footage of Indigenous peoples in archival collections, and developing free and open-source software for archivists. He has contributed to the development of vrecord, open-source software for videotape digitization.

Jonathan has worked on the collections of Anthology Film Archives, the Bancroft Library at University of California Berkeley, the Museo del Cine in Buenos Aires, City University Television (CUNY TV), and the Museum of Modern Art, and NYU Libraries. In addition, he has also assessed and provided recommendations for digital preservation at Carnegie Hall.

He was the secretary of NYU’s Association of Moving Image Archivists (AMIA) student chapter and was the recipient of AMIA’s Universal Studios Preservation Scholarship as well as the Robert Sklar Memorial Scholarship. Before starting the MIAP program, Jonathan worked as a programmer analyst at Solutions for Progress and a program associate at Scribe Video Center, both in Philadelphia. He holds a bachelor’s degree in Film from Vassar College.
