About Me

Jonathan Farbowitz is a graduate student in New York University’s Moving Image Archiving and Preservation (MIAP) program. His interests include preserving malware and other digital anomalies, archiving media related to social movements, experimental film and video preservation, and developing free and open source software for archivists. He has contributed to the development of vrecord, open-source capture software for digitizing videotapes.

Jonathan has worked on the collections of Anthology Film Archives, the Fales Library at New York University, the Bancroft Library at University of California Berkeley, Museo del Cine Pablo Ducrós Hicken in Buenos Aires, as well as City University Television, Interference Archive, and Educational Video Center in New York City. In addition, he has also assessed and provided recommendations for digital preservation at Carnegie Hall. He is the current secretary of NYU’s AMIA student chapter and was the recipient of AMIA’s Universal Studios Preservation Scholarship in 2014 as well as the Robert Sklar Memorial Scholarship in 2015. Before starting the MIAP program Jonathan worked as a programmer analyst at Solutions for Progress and a program associate at Scribe Video Center, both in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He holds a bachelor’s degree in Film from Vassar College.
